Course description

Education institutions must assess varied and extensive information to gauge institutional effectiveness and generate data-based decisions. This course helps students plan, design, and conduct diverse mixed-methods research on complicated issues in education — and solidify the data foundation needed for their capstone experience. Upon completion, students will select a preliminary capstone option and submit their decision to the School of Graduate Studies and Research.

Student learning outcomes

✓Effectively communicate mixed methods research concepts (SLO 1)
✓Apply descriptive and inferential statistics to analyze numeric data (SLO 2)
✓Develop codes, identify themes and patterns in non-numeric data (SLO 3)
✓Design and visualize empirical mixed methods research studies (SLO 4)
✓Evaluate published research using four complex mixed methods designs (SLO 5)
✓Apply ethical standards in social and educational research practice (SLO 6)
